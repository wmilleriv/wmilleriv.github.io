Probability

In this unit, students will learn to describe the likelihood of events using probabilities between 0 and 1 and find experimental probabilities, including complements and compound events. They will use experimental probability alongside proportional reasoning to make predictions about real-world situations. Students will explore theoretical probability, comparing it with experimental results, and analyze compound events using tables, tree diagrams, and organized lists. They will also design and perform simulations to test and better understand probabilities of simple and compound events.

Essential Questions

  • How can we describe the likelihood of an event using probabilities between 0 and 1?
  • What is the difference between experimental probability and theoretical probability, and how can we compare them?
  • How do we determine the probability of compound events using tables, tree diagrams, or organized lists?
  • In what ways can proportional reasoning and experimental probability be used to make predictions about real-world situations?
  • How can designing and performing simulations help us understand and test the probabilities of simple and compound events?

Unit Objectives

  • Compare the center and spread of data displayed in dot plots.
  • Compare data displayed in box plots, and use these comparisons to draw inferences about two populations.
  • Use means and MADs to compare two populations.
  • Describe the likelihood of an event in terms of a probability between 0 and 1.
  • Find the experimental probability of an event and its complement.
  • Determine the experimental probability of compound events.
  • Use experimental probability and proportional reasoning to make predictions about real-world scenarios.
  • Find the theoretical probability of simple events and compare it to experimental probability.
  • Find and compare theoretical probabilities of compound events using a table, tree diagram, or organized list.
  • Use theoretical probability and proportional reasoning to make qualitative predictions.
  • Design and perform simulations to test probabilities of simple and compound events.
