Data Analysis and Sampling

In this unit, students will learn to understand populations and how to select representative random samples to make inferences. They will explore how repeated sampling can cause variation in data and develop skills to compare the center and spread of data using dot plots and box plots. Students will use measures such as means and mean absolute deviations (MADs) to analyze and draw conclusions about two populations.

Essential Questions

  • What makes a sample representative of a population, and why is random sampling important for making valid inferences?
  • How does repeated random sampling of the same size affect the variation in data collected?
  • How can we compare the center and spread of data using dot plots and box plots to draw conclusions about populations?
  • What do measures like the mean and mean absolute deviation (MAD) tell us about the characteristics and variability of a data set?
  • How can statistical analysis of samples help us make informed decisions or predictions about larger populations?

Unit Objectives

  • Understand populations, random samples, and how to select a representative sample.
  • Use a random sample to make inferences about a population.
  • Understand that repeatedly sampling a population with the same size random sample will cause the data to vary.
  • Compare the center and spread of data displayed in dot plots.
  • Compare data displayed in box plots, and use these comparisons to draw inferences about two populations.
  • Use means and mean absolute deviations (MADs) to compare two populations.
