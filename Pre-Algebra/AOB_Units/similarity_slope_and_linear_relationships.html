Similarity, Slope, and Linear Relationships

In this unit, students will learn to use angle relationships to solve problems involving triangles, including identifying similar triangles and finding unknown angle measures. They will apply angle relationships formed by parallel lines and a transversal to determine missing values. Students will relate right triangles on the coordinate plane to linear equations, recognizing patterns in slope and proportional relationships. They will write equations of lines, interpret graphs with qualitative features, and analyze how geometric and algebraic concepts connect.

Essential Questions

  • How can angle relationships within triangles and between parallel lines and transversals help us find unknown angle measures?
  • What criteria determine whether two triangles are similar, and how can similarity be used to solve problems?
  • How are right triangles on the coordinate plane related to linear equations and proportional relationships?
  • How can we write equations of lines that represent proportional relationships and analyze their graphs?
  • In what ways do geometric concepts like similarity and angle measures connect to algebraic concepts such as slope and equations?

Unit Objectives

  • Use angle relationships in triangles.
  • Identify whether two triangles are similar, given angle measures in the triangles.
  • Find unknown angle measures in triangles known to be similar.
  • Find unknown angle measures when parallel lines are cut by a transversal.
  • Relate right triangles to the coordinates of a line going through the origin, and compare persistent features of the triangles to persistent features of the line.
  • Write the equation of a proportional relationship.
  • Write the equation of a line.
  • Sketch and analyze a graph that exhibits qualitative features.
