Transform and Construct Geometric Figures

In this unit, students will learn to derive and apply formulas for the circumference and area of a circle. They will use these formulas to calculate measurements and extend their understanding by finding the areas of composite figures. Throughout the unit, students will develop problem-solving skills related to circles and shapes composed of multiple parts.

Essential Questions

  • How can we derive the formulas for the circumference and area of a circle from its properties?
  • What strategies can we use to apply the formulas for circumference and area to solve real-world problems involving circles?
  • How do we break down composite figures into simpler shapes to find their total area accurately?
  • In what ways can understanding the properties of circles help us solve complex measurement problems?
  • How can we verify the reasonableness of our answers when calculating circumference and area in various contexts?

Unit Objectives

  • Derive and apply formulas for circumference.
  • Derive and apply formulas for the area of a circle.
  • Use known formulas to calculate the areas of composite figures.
  • Identify and describe the two-dimensional figures resulting from horizontal and vertical cross-sections of solids.
  • Calculate the surface area of a right prism using the surface area formula.
  • Calculate the volume of a right prism using the volume formula.
  • Develop and use the formula for the volume of a cylinder.
  • Develop and use the formulas for the volume of a cone and the volume of a sphere.
  • Solve multi-step problems involving three-dimensional figures using formulas for surface area and volume.
