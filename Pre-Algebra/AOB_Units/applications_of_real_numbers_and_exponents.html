Applications of Real Numbers and Exponents

In this unit, students will explore angle relationships within triangles and use these to identify similar triangles and find unknown angle measures. They will apply properties of parallel lines cut by a transversal to solve for missing angles. Connecting geometry to algebra, students will relate right triangles on the coordinate plane to the equations of lines through the origin, write equations representing proportional relationships and lines, and analyze graphs to interpret key features.

Essential Questions

  • How can angle relationships and properties of parallel lines help us identify similar triangles and find missing angle measures?
  • How do we determine whether a number is rational or irrational, and how can we order real numbers that include both types?
  • How does the Pythagorean Theorem and its converse help us solve problems involving right triangles and calculate distances on the coordinate plane?
  • What are the properties of integer exponents, and how can they be applied to simplify expressions and solve problems?
  • How can scientific notation be used to express very large or very small numbers, and what are the rules for performing operations with numbers in scientific notation?

Unit Objectives

  • Determine if a number is rational.
  • Evaluate square and cube roots.
  • Order a list of real numbers consisting of both rational and irrational numbers.
  • Prove and use the Pythagorean Theorem and its converse.
  • Use the Pythagorean Theorem to solve real-world problems involving right triangles.
  • Use the Pythagorean Theorem to find the distance between any two points in the coordinate plane.
  • Develop and use the properties of integer exponents.
  • Express numbers using scientific notation.
  • Compute with numbers written in scientific notation.
